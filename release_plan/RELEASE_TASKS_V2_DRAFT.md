# Divine Fantasy - Route-Focused Alpha Design Spec

## Vision

The first public alpha should be built and presented as a **one-week Driftwatch story-demo**.

The promise is not "huge sandbox RPG already full of content."

The promise is:

- Luke starts in a desperate position
- the intro choice is the player's first route commitment
- the Finn week is the first real test of that route
- the game is about surviving through **skill, words, or violence**

The goal of this document is not to list every alpha task.

The goal is to define how the three core routes should feel, how they should progress, how they should interact with Finn week, and what needs to be built so the demo actually delivers that fantasy.

## Core Demo Promise

**In one desperate week in Driftwatch, Luke survives through skilling, social maneuvering, or combat.**

That means the demo must deliver three sharply different approaches:

1. **Skilling Route**
2. **Social Route**
3. **Combat Route**

These routes should not only level differently.

They should solve problems differently.

The Finn debt arc should keep its current core spine, but it must become a route-aware story layer where each route has different strengths, costs, and resolutions.

## Route Design Principles

The route structure for the alpha should follow these rules:

- use **hard contrast** between routes
- do not try to equalize all routes too early
- let each route solve different player problems
- let each route feel viable, but not identical
- make the intro choice matter immediately
- make the Finn week feel like the continuation of the intro
- keep Sandbox as a temporary alpha support mode, not the long-term identity of the game

## Route 1: Skilling

### Fantasy

The skilling route is about surviving through practical competence.

The player should feel:

- poor but resourceful
- hungry but capable of stabilizing
- able to survive by working, gathering, cooking, and planning
- more efficient than powerful

This route should feel like:

- "I can make it through the week if I stay fed, work smart, and build stability."

### Core Gameplay Loop

The skilling route loop should be:

1. manage hunger, energy, and time
2. gather or work for food and basic income
3. cook or process resources into better value
4. use practical gains to stay alive and keep the Finn week moving
5. become more self-sufficient as the week continues

The route should lean on:

- fishing
- dockhand work
- gathering
- cooking
- small-scale crafting
- practical survival decisions

### Primary Resources

This route mainly spends:

- hunger
- energy
- time
- tools

This route mainly gains:

- food security
- practical materials
- modest income
- reliability
- stable day-to-day survival

### Early-Game Advantage

This route should have the clearest first-day stability.

The fishing intro should immediately give:

- a readable food route
- a way to avoid collapse
- a reason to move through the map with intent

The early benefit is not "high profit."

The early benefit is:

- "I can feed myself and stay in the game."

### Finn-Week Role

The skilling route should solve Finn-week problems through:

- labor
- supply
- survival endurance
- practical deliveries
- resource gathering
- earning by effort rather than influence or force

It should be best at:

- staying alive while pressure rises
- completing practical tasks
- solving problems through work and material competence

It should be weaker at:

- changing people's minds quickly
- forcing outcomes through intimidation or violence

### Failure Mode

This route should struggle when:

- a problem requires social leverage
- a problem requires immediate dominance
- time pressure is so strong that slow stability is not enough

Its core weakness is:

- it is dependable, but not explosive

### Required Content / Build Work

To make this route fully real in the demo:

- keep fishing as the food-security route, not the best route at everything
- keep dockhand as emergency cash, not the main debt-winning route
- strengthen gathering/cooking as practical support systems
- give Finn-week tasks that reward supply, labor, and endurance
- make skilling-based resolutions visible in quest outcomes

## Route 2: Social

### Fantasy

The social route is about surviving through people.

The player should feel:

- poor but observant
- weak in direct force, but good at reading situations
- able to survive by making contacts, persuading, leveraging trust, and gathering information

This route should feel like:

- "I get through the week because I know who to talk to, what to say, and who owes what."

### Core Gameplay Loop

The social route loop should be:

1. identify who matters
2. build trust, favors, or leverage
3. use dialogue to gain access, help, or information
4. convert relationships into survival value or Finn-week progress
5. avoid problems through soft power instead of brute force

The route should lean on:

- persuasion
- relationship shifts
- favors
- discounts
- alternative dialogue outcomes
- information gathering

### Primary Resources

This route mainly spends:

- time
- social access
- trust
- opportunities

This route mainly gains:

- information
- softer quest resolutions
- cheaper access
- support from NPCs
- alternate routes through the Finn week

### Early-Game Advantage

This route needs a much clearer first-day benefit than it has now.

The social intro must give immediate practical value such as:

- access to a helpful NPC
- reduced cost on a critical survival purchase
- a favor that converts into food, shelter, or information
- an alternate early debt-collection path that avoids grind

The key is:

- the player must feel the route is helping them survive on day one

### Finn-Week Role

The social route should solve Finn-week problems through:

- persuasion
- trust
- negotiation
- softer debt collection
- uncovering secrets
- getting people to help, reveal, or forgive

It should be best at:

- alternate resolutions
- extracting information
- reducing friction
- changing outcomes without open conflict

It should be weaker at:

- direct self-sufficiency
- winning by force

### Failure Mode

This route should struggle when:

- the player lacks useful contacts
- they need immediate food or money with no social setup
- violence or labor would have been the faster answer

Its core weakness is:

- it is flexible, but not self-sustaining by default

### Required Content / Build Work

To make this route fully real in the demo:

- expand dialogue depth significantly
- add more persuasion and leverage outcomes
- add practical social rewards, not just flavor text
- let social choices alter prices, help, access, and Finn progress
- create at least a few social-first resolutions inside the Finn week

## Route 3: Combat

### Fantasy

The combat route is about surviving through danger, intimidation, and force.

The player should feel:

- physically vulnerable at the start but willing to push risk
- able to get through the week by threatening, fighting, or taking dangerous work
- less stable than the skilling route, but more explosive

This route should feel like:

- "I survive because I am willing to do what safer people avoid."

### Core Gameplay Loop

The combat route loop should be:

1. seek high-risk opportunities
2. use attack, intimidation, or confrontation to force progress
3. take damage and danger in exchange for speed or payoff
4. convert risk into money, loot, fear, or access
5. push through Finn-week problems by force when words or labor are too slow

The route should lean on:

- combat
- intimidation
- coercive dialogue
- risky side events
- violent or threatening resolutions

### Primary Resources

This route mainly spends:

- HP
- food
- recovery time
- equipment durability in a future sense, if added later

This route mainly gains:

- forced access
- threat-based progress
- fast payoff
- danger-driven identity

### Early-Game Advantage

This route also needs a clearer first-day purpose than it has now.

The combat intro reward must provide immediate value such as:

- a more useful early combat opportunity
- a coercive debt path
- an intimidation shortcut
- access to a risky but rewarding early encounter

The player must feel:

- this route is dangerous, but it can solve problems faster

### Finn-Week Role

The combat route should solve Finn-week problems through:

- threat
- forced compliance
- violent protection or retaliation
- dangerous confrontation
- coercive debt collection

It should be best at:

- forcing progress
- bypassing soft resistance
- winning outcomes that social or skilling would reach more slowly

It should be weaker at:

- stability
- efficiency
- low-risk survival

### Failure Mode

This route should struggle when:

- the player lacks food, recovery, or gear
- problems punish reckless escalation
- the route burns too many resources too quickly

Its core weakness is:

- it solves problems fast, but it is expensive and dangerous

### Required Content / Build Work

To make this route fully real in the demo:

- expand combat situations beyond basic fights
- add more intimidation/coercion uses in dialogue
- create a clearer "risky but useful" path through early Finn-week content
- add combat-linked payoffs that justify choosing danger
- ensure combat is useful, but not the automatic strongest route

## Finn Week Route Layer

### Core Rule

The current Finn debt arc should **keep its existing quest spine**.

This is not a full rewrite.

Instead, the Finn week should gain a route layer where each major problem can be approached differently depending on the route the player is leaning into.

### Finn Week Structure Goal

The week should stop feeling like a mostly fixed puzzle.

It should feel like:

- the intro choice echoes into the main story
- the player's route changes how they survive, collect, negotiate, and escalate
- different routes see different strengths, costs, and consequences

### Route-Layer Rules

For major Finn-week problems:

- **Skilling** resolves through labor, supply, endurance, practical hustles, and self-sufficiency
- **Social** resolves through persuasion, favors, relationships, information, and soft leverage
- **Combat** resolves through threat, force, intimidation, risky confrontation, and dangerous opportunity

### Required Outcome Standard

Each important Finn-week problem should ideally support:

- one skilling-forward resolution
- one social-forward resolution
- one combat-forward resolution

Routes can overlap.

One route can sometimes still use another route's solution.

But each scene or problem should have one route that feels especially natural and especially rewarding.

### Demo Ending Goal

The end of the demo should feel like:

- a turning point
- shaped by how the player approached the week
- enough closure to satisfy the demo
- enough open tension to make the player want to continue

## Sandbox Role During Alpha

Sandbox should remain in the alpha, but only as a **temporary support mode**.

Its role is:

- systems exploration
- economy testing
- freeplay support
- seeing more of Driftwatch without the same pressure

Its role is not:

- the long-term identity of the game
- the main promise of the alpha
- a replacement for the route-focused Story Mode

For the long-term game vision:

- Story should continue naturally after Finn
- long-form economy play should happen inside the real campaign
- a dedicated separate Sandbox mode should not be treated as the final structure forever

## Build Order

Implementation should follow this order:

1. **Define route fantasy and route identity**
   - lock what skilling, social, and combat are supposed to feel like
   - keep hard contrast between them

2. **Fix intro rewards so each route has immediate first-day value**
   - fishing already has a real survival hook
   - social and combat need equally practical early hooks

3. **Expand the social route until it is mechanically real**
   - dialogue depth
   - practical outcomes
   - survival help through people, not only flavor

4. **Expand the combat route until it is mechanically real**
   - more useful combat opportunities
   - intimidation/coercion use cases
   - clearer risk-for-reward structure

5. **Add Finn-week route branches and alternate resolutions**
   - keep the current spine
   - layer route-specific solutions on top of it

6. **Rebalance the week around the new route structure**
   - economy
   - survival pressure
   - first 1-2 days
   - route parity

7. **Keep Sandbox only as an alpha support mode**
   - useful for testing
   - not marketed as the main identity

## Current Completed Foundations

Already completed and supporting this direction:

- Luke base stats changed to `STR 3 / DEX 4 / INT 6 / CHA 2`
- Luke now starts with `0` copper
- Luke now starts with no default items
- Story Mode intro now starts comfortable, then drops Luke to `20 hunger` when the Finn phase begins
- Story Mode start changed to `80 energy`
- Sandbox start now overrides to `100 energy / 100 hunger`
- Hidden dev seed of starting `wolf_pelt` items removed
- Intro fishing choice grants `Fishing level 3 + fishing rod`
- Character Selection reflects the new Luke stats
- Character attribute descriptors were cleaned up and made more consistent
- Raw fish and raw meat are blocked from direct use
- `stale_bread` was added as a poverty-tier emergency food
- The food ladder was reworked so bought food is harsher and cooked food matters more
- Dockhand pay was reduced from `20` to `18`
- Tavern and fish shop pricing was adjusted for the early survival loop
- Fishing progression was rebalanced:
- docks now scale sardine catch chance by Fishing level
- river trout now requires Fishing `5+`
- river pike now requires Fishing `7+`
- river catch rates are lower, making it a progression spot instead of an early exploit

## Release Standard

The first public alpha should make players think:

**"This is early, but it already knows what kind of RPG it wants to be."**
