# Divine Fantasy - Social NPC Breakdown

## Purpose

This document applies the social system to the actual early alpha NPC cast.

It focuses on:

- Finn-week core NPCs
- Roberta as the first strong social-route support NPC
- Old Leo as the route-defining intro/anchor NPC
- clue NPCs that matter for social progression and rebel-path discovery

This is not final dialogue writing.

It is a route-design breakdown for what each NPC should do in the social system.

## Covered NPCs

### Core Finn-Week NPCs

- Finn
- Beryl
- Ben
- Elara
- Elisa

### Route Support NPCs

- Roberta
- Old Leo

### Clue / Social-Leverage NPCs

- Old Crank
- Shaky Jace

## Global Rules For This Set

- major NPCs should support optional authored `first_meet_node`
- `Ask` is for discovery
- `Quest` is for progression
- `Friendly`, `Flirt`, and `Coerce` should be authored, not spam loops
- Finn-week NPCs should expose at least one social-first resolution path
- route clues should be distributed across NPCs, not concentrated in one character

## 1. Old Leo

### Role

Old Leo is the route-defining intro NPC and Luke's emotional baseline.

He should:

- frame the route choice
- give early identity to Luke
- provide lore and emotional grounding
- later serve as a safe social memory figure rather than a hard progression NPC

### Social Class / Personality

- class: working / caretaker
- personality: warm, dutiful, observant

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- `Friendly`
- `Quest` limited
- no `Flirt`
- no `Coerce`

### First-Meet Node

Yes, authored.

The intro node already effectively serves this function and should remain fully authored.

### Ask Topics

Early:

- the lighthouse
- the Sons of the Moon
- Luke's behavior / expectations

Later:

- Driftwatch history
- moral advice
- memory and belonging

### Friendly Use

Old Leo should be a low-risk social-learning NPC.

Friendly actions here should:

- help teach the social system
- slightly improve friendship
- give Luke one emotionally safe place in the route

### Quest Use

Leo should not become a major Finn-week solver.

He should instead:

- give guidance
- reflect on Luke's route
- reinforce the emotional meaning of choices

### Social Route Purpose

Old Leo teaches:

- social routes are not only manipulation
- information and kindness matter
- the intro choice is part of Luke's identity

## 2. Roberta

### Role

Roberta is the best early social-route support NPC and should become the player's first proof that social play is real.

She should:

- reward information
- reward friendship
- open one practical nonviolent support route
- bridge social play with skilling and survival

### Social Class / Personality

- class: merchant
- personality: guarded, practical, lonely

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- `Friendly`
- `Flirt`
- `Quest`
- limited `Coerce`

### First-Meet Node

Yes, authored.

Goals:

- establish Roberta as capable but burdened
- let Luke make a first impression
- seed her connection to practical survival and the docks

### Ask Topics

Should include:

- Tide & Trade history
- her family
- storm damage
- the docks
- local gossip later

### Friendly Use

Roberta should respond best to:

- `Offer Help`
- `Listen`
- `Be Honest`

She should respond poorly to:

- empty flattery
- hard pressure too early

### Flirt Use

Roberta is one of the few valid early flirt-capable NPCs.

Flirt here should:

- be low-key at first
- require some friendship first
- feel warmer and more human, not instantly romantic

### Quest Use

Her wall quest should become the model for social quest structure:

- `Ask` discovers the problem
- `Quest` carries the actual progression

### Social Route Purpose

Roberta proves that:

- friendship can unlock practical value
- social route is not just lore
- relationship building can open real survival support

## 3. Finn

### Role

Finn is the central pressure NPC.

He should not feel socially open.

He should feel like:

- a power figure
- a threat
- a man Luke can learn from, fear, manipulate, or eventually challenge

### Social Class / Personality

- class: criminal
- personality: proud, dangerous, controlling, perceptive

### Menu Categories

- `Ask` limited
- `Friendly` very limited
- `Coerce`
- `Quest`
- no `Flirt`

### First-Meet Node

Yes, authored.

The debt intro already fills this role and should remain authored.

### Ask Topics

Finn's Ask menu should be highly controlled.

Good uses:

- the debt job
- the Guild
- rules and expectations
- hints about his worldview

Ask should not make Finn feel casually available.

### Friendly Use

Finn should not have normal warmth-building friendship scenes.

If `Friendly` exists for him, it should mean:

- deference
- respect
- careful rapport

Not:

- comfort
- intimacy

### Coerce Use

Finn is one of the most important coercion-sensitive NPCs.

This should not mean Luke easily intimidates Finn early.

Instead it should support:

- bold pushback later
- hard route branching
- dangerous escalation

### Quest Use

Finn's Quest branch remains the spine of the week.

The social route should add:

- more questions that reveal motives and contradictions
- more opportunities to read how he judges Luke
- route-shaped ways to challenge or negotiate with him later

### Social Route Purpose

Finn should prove:

- social route is not only about kindness
- social route also means reading power, fear, and leverage

## 4. Beryl

### Role

Beryl is a strong mixed social NPC because he supports:

- soft approach
- coercive approach
- blackmail approach
- practical quest workaround

He is one of the best examples of how social route can branch.

### Social Class / Personality

- class: merchant
- personality: anxious, weak-willed, self-protective, compromising

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- `Friendly`
- `Quest`
- `Coerce`
- no `Flirt`

### First-Meet Node

Yes, authored.

Goals:

- establish him as nervous and compromised
- make him feel like someone with secrets
- support both soft and hard routes later

### Ask Topics

Should include:

- business trouble
- the shop
- money problems
- nerves / pressure

Potential unlocks:

- package route
- suspicion that he is hiding something

### Friendly Use

Beryl should respond to:

- reassurance
- practical empathy
- nonthreatening offers

This can open:

- softer debt handling
- package-delivery route

### Coerce Use

Beryl should be a major coercion test NPC.

This includes:

- direct threat route
- letter blackmail route
- extracting extra money at social cost

### Quest Use

Beryl should support all of these:

- direct payment demand
- soft workaround
- blackmail leverage
- social compromise branch

### Social Route Purpose

Beryl proves:

- social route can be soft or predatory
- information can be more valuable than raw force
- clue-finding matters

## 5. Ben

### Role

Ben is the "bad influence" debtor.

He represents:

- charm without responsibility
- temptation into weak or dishonest solutions
- route overlap between social and shady survival

### Social Class / Personality

- class: poor / working
- personality: slippery, impulsive, opportunistic, friendly when useful

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- limited `Friendly`
- limited `Coerce`
- `Quest`
- no `Flirt`

### First-Meet Node

Optional authored node recommended.

Goals:

- make him feel immediately unreliable but likable
- suggest that he survives through improvisation and shortcuts

### Ask Topics

Should include:

- the farm
- money problems
- drinking / gambling habits
- his excuses

### Friendly Use

Friendly here should not simply mean "Ben likes you."

It should mean:

- he talks more
- he lets Luke in on shady plans
- he becomes easier to manipulate or side with

### Coerce Use

Ben should respond to fear more than respect.

Coercion here could push:

- quicker admission
- more direct pressure
- harsher debt resolution

### Quest Use

Ben's route should remain morally messy.

The social route here should allow:

- understanding his weakness
- choosing whether to indulge or shut him down
- possibly unlocking a cleaner alternative later if expanded

### Social Route Purpose

Ben proves:

- social route includes reading weak men and opportunists
- not every social solution is clean or noble

## 6. Elisa

### Role

Elisa is the emotional collateral around Ben's debt.

She helps the route show:

- the cost of coercion
- the social meaning of household debt
- the difference between fear-based and sympathy-based play

### Social Class / Personality

- class: poor
- personality: protective, exhausted, fearful, resentful

### Menu Categories

- `Ask` limited
- `Friendly` limited
- `Coerce`
- `Quest`
- no `Flirt`

### First-Meet Node

Optional authored node recommended if she becomes more important.

### Ask Topics

Could include:

- Ben's habits
- the farm household
- what she has already lost

### Friendly Use

Friendly should be difficult but meaningful.

If Luke approaches her with sympathy, this could eventually support:

- more context
- a softer route
- guilt framing around Ben

### Coerce Use

Her current content already fits the coercive route strongly.

This should remain:

- emotionally ugly
- effective but costly

### Quest Use

Elisa's social role is less about long quest chains and more about:

- consequence
- fear
- moral pressure

### Social Route Purpose

Elisa proves:

- fear works
- but it leaves scars
- social route includes emotional damage, not just manipulation success

## 7. Elara

### Role

Elara is one of the best social-route Finn NPCs because she supports:

- soft arrangement
- secret discovery
- blackmail
- moral ambiguity

### Social Class / Personality

- class: poor / healer / criminal-adjacent
- personality: guarded, practical, secretive, compassionate in selective ways

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- `Friendly`
- `Quest`
- limited `Coerce`
- no `Flirt`

### First-Meet Node

Yes, recommended.

Goals:

- establish her as calm but guarded
- suggest hidden layers
- make her hovel feel like a social/intellectual route space

### Ask Topics

Should include:

- herbs and healing
- neighborhood hardship
- her nighttime visitors
- her risk with guards

### Friendly Use

Elara should respond well to:

- restraint
- tact
- practical empathy

This can open:

- delivery route
- cleaner debt outcome
- more nuanced secrets

### Coerce Use

Elara should support:

- blackmail
- hard pressure once secret is known

This should remain useful, but clearly more morally corrosive than the soft path.

### Quest Use

Elara is a great route-design template because she supports:

- soft deal
- secret discovery
- coercive payoff

### Social Route Purpose

Elara proves:

- social route can move through secrecy, discretion, and leverage
- information can create both humane and exploitative outcomes

## 8. Old Crank

### Role

Old Crank is a clue-delivery NPC and should remain one of the strongest `Ask` examples in the alpha.

He is useful because he already models:

- info for payment
- gossip progression
- rebel-path clue delivery

### Social Class / Personality

- class: poor / tavern drifter
- personality: bitter, talkative when bribed, sharp-eyed

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- limited `Friendly`
- `Quest` limited
- no `Flirt`
- no `Coerce` for the first pass

### First-Meet Node

Optional.

### Ask Use

He should become one of the clearest examples of:

- paying for information
- route clue chaining
- social route using curiosity and persistence

### Social Route Purpose

Old Crank proves:

- not all social progression comes from liking people
- some of it comes from managing weird, low-status informants

## 9. Shaky Jace

### Role

Shaky Jace is a clue NPC for Elara's secret.

He is useful because he shows:

- social route can use very minor NPCs
- information can be bought cheaply
- route clues do not need major scenes to matter

### Social Class / Personality

- class: poor / addict
- personality: desperate, unstable, needy

### Menu Categories

- `Ask`
- maybe very limited `Friendly`
- no `Flirt`
- no `Coerce` in first pass
- no real `Quest`

### First-Meet Node

Not needed.

### Ask Use

He should remain a focused information NPC:

- one practical social clue
- one social/economic choice
- one meaningful route payoff

### Social Route Purpose

Jace proves:

- even tiny NPCs can matter in the social route
- social route is partly about noticing who everyone else ignores

## Cross-NPC Finn-Week Mapping

### Social Route Through Finn Week

The social route through the current Finn cast should feel like:

- Leo frames Luke's identity and worldview
- Roberta proves friendship can lead to practical value
- Ben shows weakness and manipulation
- Elisa shows fear and moral cost
- Beryl shows soft leverage vs blackmail
- Elara shows secrecy and tact
- Old Crank and Jace reveal clue-based alternate routes
- Finn becomes the final test of whether Luke learned to read power

## Build Priority For These NPCs

Implement in this order:

1. Old Leo
   - route-defining authored intro and safe social structure
2. Roberta
   - first true social support NPC
3. Beryl
   - best mixed social/coercive route example
4. Elara
   - best secret/discretion route example
5. Finn
   - central power NPC
6. Ben and Elisa
   - weaker but important moral-pressure branch
7. Old Crank and Shaky Jace
   - clue-support NPCs

## Success Standard

This NPC breakdown is successful if:

- each important Finn-week NPC has a clear social role
- social route meaningfully changes how Luke deals with the debt week
- Roberta and Leo anchor the humane side of the route
- Beryl and Elara anchor leverage and secrecy
- Finn anchors pressure and power
- Ben/Elisa show moral messiness
- clue NPCs support alternate path discovery without bloating scope
